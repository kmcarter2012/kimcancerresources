Illinois Resources

Explore a variety of programs and services available specifically for Illinois residents.

Illinois Resource Search Portals

  • OneCHIFAM Resource Portal: A unified platform to search for health and social services in Cook County and surrounding regions via Unite Us.
  • 211 Metro Chicago Resource Search: Find food, utility, housing, and other community assistance programs across the Chicago metro area.
  • Illinois DHS Find Help: Official Illinois Department of Human Services portal to locate benefits and support services statewide.

Illinois Housing Resources

  • Illinois Department of Human Services: Provides information on homeless prevention and support services for Illinois residents.
  • Illinois Housing Development Authority: Offers resources for affordable rental housing throughout Illinois.
  • DCEO Housing Assistance: Provides various housing assistance programs through the Illinois Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity.
  • Illinois Housing Help: Offers resources for rental assistance, mortgage assistance, and homelessness prevention.
  • ILHousingSearch.org: Phone: (877) 428-8844. Search tool for affordable, subsidized, and market-rate units.

Illinois Utility Assistance

  • DCEO Utility Bill Assistance: Information on Illinois programs that help with utility bills through the Department of Commerce and Economic Opportunity.
  • How to Apply for Utility Assistance: Step-by-step guidance on applying for utility bill assistance in Illinois.
  • Home Weatherization & Community Action Agencies: Resources for home weatherization assistance and local community action agencies that can help.
  • CEDA Gas and Electric Assistance: The Community and Economic Development Association offers help with gas and electric bills for eligible residents.
  • Help Illinois Families: Provides information about various assistance programs for Illinois families, including utility assistance.
  • Catholic Charities: Offers various assistance programs including utility bill help for those in need.
  • USA.gov Help with Energy Bills: Federal resources for energy bill assistance that Illinois residents can access.

Illinois Food & Nutrition Resources

  • Illinois DHS – Apply for Benefits: Apply for SNAP, medical, and cash assistance through the Department of Human Services.
  • ABE Illinois (Application for Benefits Eligibility): Use this online portal to apply for SNAP (food stamps), medical, and other public benefits.
  • Illinois.gov – Apply for SNAP and Medical Assistance: Direct access to state services for food and healthcare benefits.
  • Northern Illinois Food Bank – SNAP Help: Offers help applying for SNAP benefits and information on local food resources.
  • We Got You Illinois – SNAP Support: A campaign helping individuals understand and enroll in SNAP food benefits.
  • Illinois Department on Aging – Nutrition Programs: Statewide senior nutrition services including home-delivered and group meals.
  • Illinois DHS – Food Assistance Programs: Overview of food-related programs including SNAP and child nutrition.
  • Illinois DHS – Nutrition Programs for Women, Infants, and Children (WIC): Resources for WIC services across the state for pregnant women, infants, and children under five.
  • Illinois DHS – Emergency Food Program: Provides emergency food support through local food pantries and community agencies.
  • Eat Move Save Illinois Food Finder: Find local food resources and healthy eating tips.

Cook County / Chicago Resources

Explore comprehensive support services available to residents of Chicago and Cook County.

General Assistance & Search Portals

  • City of Chicago – Resident Resources: Comprehensive guide to housing, food, healthcare, and financial assistance programs.
  • 211 Metro Chicago: Connects residents to essential health and social services, including shelter, food, and mental health support.
  • DFSS Community Service Centers: Six centers offering in-person benefit assessments, shelter, food, clothing, and job training. Open Monday–Friday, 9am–5pm.

Mental Health Services

  • NAMI Chicago: A nonprofit that operates a free and confidential hotline staffed by clinicians and peers who can help you find treatment and provide free resources to those in need of mental health support. To reach the hotline, call 1.800.950-NAMI.
  • Find other mental health resources here: Visit this link to find additional mental health support.

Services for People with Disabilities

  • Mayor's Office for People with Disabilities (MOPD): Can offer personal assistant services, assistive devices, home-delivered meals, home modification and benefits counseling for people with disabilities living in the City of Chicago under the age of 60. To learn more about services offered, call 312-744-7050 or visit the MOPD website.

Transportation Resources

  • Cook County Department of Transportation and Highways Senior Services: Provides transportation resources and services specifically for seniors throughout Cook County.
  • Chicago Department of Family & Support Services Senior Services Division: Offers transportation assistance and referrals for seniors residing in Chicago.
  • Regional Transportation Authority (RTA) Reduced Fare and Ride Free Programs: Administers programs allowing seniors 65+ to ride for free or at reduced rates on CTA, Metra, and Pace.
  • Chicago Transit Authority (CTA) Accessibility Services: Provides accessible transportation options for seniors and individuals with disabilities.
  • Pace Paratransit Services: Offers door-to-door transportation services for eligible seniors and people with disabilities.
  • Cook County Dial-a-Ride Programs: Provides on-demand, shared-ride services in various Cook County communities.
  • AgeOptions Transportation Assistance Programs: Connects seniors to transportation resources through the suburban Cook County Area Agency on Aging.
  • Catholic Charities Elder Services Transportation: Offers transportation assistance for seniors to medical appointments, grocery shopping, and other essential needs.
  • North Shore Senior Center Escorted Transportation Service: Provides volunteer-based transportation for seniors in northern Cook County suburbs.
  • Kenneth Young Center Senior Transportation: Offers transportation services for seniors in northwest suburban Cook County.
  • Pace Suburban Bus: Phone: (800) 606-1282. Provides paratransit services for people with disabilities in Cook County who are unable to use regular fixed-route public transportation.
  • Chicago Transit Authority (CTA): Phone: (888) 968-7282. Offers ADA Paratransit Service for people with disabilities in Chicago and suburban Cook County, providing transportation to medical appointments, work, and other necessary destinations.
  • Senior Ride Program: Phone: (312) 744-4016. A service provided by the City of Chicago Department of Family and Support Services offering free rides for seniors aged 60+ who live in Chicago for medical appointments, grocery shopping, and other necessary destinations.
  • Township Senior Services: Many townships in Cook County offer transportation services for seniors. For example, Evanston Township Senior Services provides free transportation for seniors aged 60+ who live in Evanston.
  • Ride Free Illinois: Allows eligible seniors and people with disabilities to ride public transportation for free. To qualify, individuals must be aged 65+, have a disability, or have a Medicare card.
  • ModivCare: Phone: 1-877-831-3438. Provides non-emergency medical transportation services for Blue Cross Community Health Plans members. Available 24/7 with 3 business days advance scheduling.
  • Calumet City Temporary Senior Transportation Program: Phone: (708) 891-8190. Offers transportation for eligible seniors residing in Calumet City for essential trips such as medical appointments, grocery shopping, and other necessary services.

  • Commodity Supplemental Food Program: Provides nutritious USDA food packages to low-income Illinois seniors aged 60+. Participants receive various foods including cereal, milk, cheese, canned meat, fruits, vegetables, and more.
  • Greater Chicago Food Depository: Address: 4100 West Ann Lurie Place, Chicago, IL 60632. Phone: 773-247-3663. Email: olderadults@gcfd.org. Find a food pantry, soup kitchen, shelter or mobile program in your community by calling 773.247.3663 or visiting: https://www.chicagosfoodbank.org/find-food/. Counties served: Cook.
  • Public Assistance: Women, Infants and Children (WIC): Helps pregnant women, new mothers and young children eat well and stay healthy. Find out if you are eligible here: https://wic.fns.usda.gov/wps/pages/preScreenTool.xhtml.
