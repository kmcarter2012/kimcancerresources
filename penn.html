Pennsylvania Statewide Resources

Explore a variety of programs and services available specifically for Pennsylvania residents.

Pennsylvania Resource Search Portals

  • PA 211: Pennsylvania's statewide resource hotline and website connecting residents to health and human service needs. Call 211 for assistance.
  • COMPASS PA: Apply for health care, food stamps, cash assistance, and many other services through this online portal.
  • COMPASS Benefits Portal: Online application for Pennsylvanians to apply for many health and human service programs.
  • FindHelp.org Pennsylvania: Search for free and reduced-cost services in your area across Pennsylvania.
  • Pennsylvania Area Agencies on Aging: Find your local Area Agency on Aging for senior services.

Pennsylvania Housing Resources

  • Pennsylvania Housing Finance Agency (PHFA): Provides affordable housing options, homeowner resources, and rental assistance programs statewide.
  • PA DHS Housing Resources: Housing assistance information through the Department of Human Services.
  • PHFA Housing Programs for Persons with Disabilities: Information on housing resources for individuals with disabilities.
  • Pennsylvania Emergency Rental Assistance Program: Helps eligible renters affected by financial hardship pay for rent and utilities.
  • Housing Counseling Agencies: Find free housing counseling services by county.
  • Supportive Housing Programs: Assistance for vulnerable populations including those with disabilities or special needs.
  • PEAL Center Housing Resources: Housing information for families of children and adults with disabilities.

Pennsylvania Utility Assistance

  • Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP): Helps low-income households with heating bills. Apply through COMPASS or your County Assistance Office.
  • LIHEAP Crisis Program: Emergency assistance for utility shutoffs or heating system failures.
  • Pennsylvania Public Utility Commission - Customer Assistance Programs: Information on various utility assistance programs including CAP, LIURP, and CARES.
  • Dollar Energy Fund: Provides utility assistance to low-income households through the Hardship Program.
  • Electric League of Pennsylvania Assistance: Connect to energy assistance programs throughout Pennsylvania.
  • United Way of Pennsylvania Utility Help: Statewide utility assistance resources and referrals.
  • Pennsylvania Assistance Programs: Comprehensive list of assistance options for utilities and other basic needs.
  • PECO Customer Assistance Programs: Help for PECO customers who are having difficulty paying their bills.
  • PGW Customer Assistance: Payment assistance programs for Philadelphia Gas Works customers.

Pennsylvania Food & Nutrition Resources

  • Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP): Food assistance benefits for eligible low-income individuals and families.
  • COMPASS - Apply for SNAP: Online portal to apply for SNAP benefits.
  • Feeding Pennsylvania: Find food banks and pantries in your area.
  • Women, Infants, and Children (WIC): Nutrition program for eligible pregnant women, new mothers, and young children.
  • Senior Farmers Market Nutrition Program: Provides eligible seniors with vouchers to purchase fresh produce.
  • PA Department of Aging Nutrition Services: Information on congregate meals and home-delivered meals for seniors.
  • Hunger-Free PA Resources: Comprehensive directory of food assistance programs.
  • Philabundance: Food assistance in the Philadelphia region and Delaware Valley.
  • Greater Pittsburgh Community Food Bank: Food assistance in southwestern Pennsylvania.
  • School Meals Programs: Information on school breakfast, lunch, and summer food service programs.

Pennsylvania Senior Resources

Explore senior-focused services including benefits, caregiving support, and local aging resources.

General Senior Services & Hotlines

  • Pennsylvania Department of Aging: Statewide agency overseeing services for older Pennsylvanians.
  • Area Agencies on Aging: Find your local AAA for senior services in your county.
  • PA Caregiver Support Program: Assistance for family caregivers of older adults.
  • Older Adult Protective Services: Investigates reports of elder abuse and neglect. Report elder abuse 24/7 at 1-800-490-8505.
  • PA MEDI (formerly APPRISE) Helpline: Medicare counseling at 1-800-783-7067.
  • PA Aging and Disability Resource Helpline: Call 1-800-753-8827 for resources and support.
  • Long-Term Care Ombudsman Hotline: Call 717-783-7247 for concerns about long-term care.

Senior Benefits and Financial Assistance

  • Property Tax/Rent Rebate Program: Rebates up to $650 for eligible seniors 65+, widows/widowers 50+, and disabled 18+. Hotline: 1-888-222-9190.
  • LIHEAP for Seniors: Energy assistance with special eligibility considerations for seniors.
  • PACE/PACENET: Prescription assistance programs for Pennsylvania seniors 65+.
  • SNAP Benefits for Seniors: Food assistance with special rules for older adults.
  • Attendant Care Services: Financial support for in-home care services.
  • Options Program: In-home services for seniors who don't qualify for Medicaid.
  • Utility Customer Assistance Programs: Reduced rates and payment plans for eligible seniors.

Senior Healthcare Resources

  • PA Department of Health Senior Programs: Health services and chronic disease management.
  • Medical Assistance (Medicaid): Health coverage for eligible low-income seniors.
  • Pennie Health Insurance: Pennsylvania's official health insurance marketplace for those under 65.
  • Health & Wellness Programs: Evidence-based programs for chronic disease management and fall prevention.
  • Community HealthChoices (CHC): Managed care program for dual-eligible Medicare/Medicaid recipients.

Senior Housing and Living Arrangements

  • PHFA Senior Housing Options: Pennsylvania Housing Finance Agency resources for senior housing.
  • Personal Care Homes: Residential facilities offering assistance with daily living.
  • Shared Housing and Resources Program (SHARE): Matching homeowners with home seekers for shared living arrangements.
  • PHFA Home Repair Programs: Assistance for home modifications and repairs for older homeowners.
  • Nursing Facility Information: Resources and information on skilled nursing facilities.
  • Senior Housing Counseling: Free counseling services to help seniors explore housing options.
  • Independent Enrollment Broker: Help with long-term care service enrollment.

Senior Technology and Education

  • Senior Community Center Services: Educational programs and technology training at local centers.
  • AARP Pennsylvania: Technology education, workshops, and resources for seniors.
  • PA TellNet: Pennsylvania's Technology Enhanced Lifelong Learning Network.
  • Presbyterian Senior Living Learning Opportunities: Educational programs throughout Pennsylvania.
  • Osher Lifelong Learning Institute (University of Pittsburgh): Educational programs for adults 50+.
  • Temple University OLLI: Learning opportunities for older adults in Philadelphia.

Senior Legal Resources

  • PA Legal Aid Network: Free legal assistance for eligible seniors throughout Pennsylvania.
  • SeniorLAW Center: Legal services for older Pennsylvanians. Statewide Helpline: 877-727-7529.
  • PA Law Help: Free legal information for low-income Pennsylvanians.
  • Elder Abuse Prevention & Reporting: Legal protections and reporting procedures.
