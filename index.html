National Resources

Explore a variety of national programs and services available across the United States.

Healthcare & Medication Assistance

  • HealthCare.gov: The federal government's health insurance marketplace offering access to affordable healthcare coverage and enrollment information.
  • HRSA Health Center Finder: Locate federally funded health centers offering low-cost or free medical, dental, and mental health care based on income and need.
  • CancerCare: Offers financial assistance, support groups, and resources for cancer patients and caregivers.
  • NeedyMeds: Provides information on programs that help with the cost of medications and healthcare.
  • RxAssist: A directory of patient assistance programs for affordable medications.
  • GoodRx: Helps individuals find discounts and pricing for prescription medications.
  • TogetherRxAccess: Offers discounts on prescription medications for qualifying individuals.
  • SingleCare: Provides discounts and coupons to reduce the cost of prescriptions at participating pharmacies.
  • Medicare Extra Help (Low-Income Subsidy): Assists eligible Medicare beneficiaries with limited income and resources in paying prescription drug costs.
  • Mark Cuban Cost Plus Drug Company: Offers generic medications at significantly reduced prices by eliminating pharmacy benefit managers and middlemen.
  • NeedyMeds Free Clinic Finder: Database of free, low-cost, and sliding scale clinics searchable by location.
  • 340B OPAIS Covered Entity Search: Locate healthcare facilities that participate in the 340B Drug Pricing Program, providing medications at significantly reduced prices.
  • Medicare Care Compare: Find and compare healthcare providers, home health agencies, hospitals, and nursing homes based on quality ratings.
  • State Pharmaceutical Assistance Programs: State-run programs that help eligible residents with prescription drug costs.
  • Medicaid Contact Information: Resources for contacting Medicaid about healthcare coverage and benefits.
  • Social Security Administration Contact: Contact information for Social Security programs including disability.
  • USA.gov Social Security Disability: Information about applying for and receiving Social Security disability benefits.
  • BenefitsCheckUp: Helps seniors with limited income find programs that may help pay for healthcare costs and medications.
  • Partnership for Prescription Assistance: Helps qualifying patients without prescription drug coverage access needed medicines through patient assistance programs.
  • Medicine Assistance Tool: Search engine that helps patients find financial assistance programs for their medications.
  • Patient Access Network Foundation: Provides financial assistance for out-of-pocket costs associated with prescribed medications.
  • Patient Advocate Foundation: Offers case management services and financial aid to patients with chronic, life-threatening, or debilitating conditions.
  • HealthWell Foundation: Provides financial assistance for medications, health insurance premiums, and other healthcare costs.
  • The Assistance Fund: Helps patients with chronic and serious illnesses pay for their medications and treatments.
  • 211.org: Connects people to local healthcare services and other resources.
  • FamilyWize: Partners with pharmacies nationwide to negotiate prescription discounts.
  • Free Clinic Directory: National database of free and charitable clinics for the uninsured and underinsured.
  • NHSC Site Finder: Locates healthcare facilities with National Health Service Corps clinicians providing care in underserved areas.
  • Indian Health Service Facility Locator: Helps Native Americans and Alaska Natives find IHS, Tribal, and Urban Indian health centers.
  • National Association of Free & Charitable Clinics: Helps locate free clinics across the country.
  • HRSA Mobile Health Center Finder: Locates mobile clinics that provide healthcare services to underserved areas.
  • HUD Housing Choice Voucher Program: Information about housing assistance, which can impact health outcomes and healthcare access.
  • Critical Access Hospitals: Information about hospitals serving rural communities with limited healthcare options.
  • Am I Rural? Tool: Determines if a location is considered rural for various federal healthcare programs and funding opportunities.
  • NIDDK Health Information Clearinghouses: Free health information about diabetes, kidney, digestive diseases, and related conditions.

Mental Health, Substance Abuse, and Tobacco Cessation Resources

Access national hotlines, treatment locators, peer recovery programs, and quitting support for mental health, alcohol, drugs, and tobacco.

Mental Health & Crisis Support

  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988 for 24/7, free, and confidential crisis support.
  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI): Offers mental health education, peer support groups, and advocacy nationwide.
  • NAMI National Helpline: Call 1-800-950-NAMI (6264), text "Helpline" to 62640, or email helpline@nami.org for support.
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 to connect with a trained volunteer crisis counselor.
  • Seize the Awkward: Helps young people start conversations about mental health with friends and peers.
  • Prevention First: Offers behavioral health training, prevention strategies, and substance misuse resources in Illinois.
  • FindTreatment.gov: A confidential and anonymous resource for locating mental health and substance use treatment services across the U.S.
  • Mental Health America – Finding Help: Guidance on how to find help for mental health concerns, including support groups and affordable care.
  • Psychology Today Therapist Finder: Search for licensed therapists in your area by specialty, insurance, and more.
  • SAMHSA Find Help: Federal resource hub for mental health, substance abuse, and behavioral health services.
  • Veterans Crisis Line: Call 988 then press 1, or text 838255, for 24/7 support for veterans and their families.
  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357) — a free, confidential, 24/7 treatment referral and information service.
  • Disaster Distress Helpline: Call or text 1-800-985-5990 for crisis counseling related to natural or human-caused disasters.
  • SAMHSA ESMI Treatment Locator: Helps locate early serious mental illness (ESMI) programs in your area.

Substance Use and Recovery Services

  • FindTreatment.gov: National database to locate substance use and mental health treatment services. Call 1-800-662-HELP (4357).
  • SAMHSA National Helpline: 24/7 free and confidential help for individuals and families facing substance abuse or mental health challenges.
  • Illinois Helpline for Opioids & Other Substances: Free, confidential support and referrals. Call 1-833-2FINDHELP.
  • Illinois DHS Substance Use Disorder Services: Access statewide treatment programs and licensed providers.
  • Narcotics Anonymous (NA): Assists people recovering from prescription or illicit drug use. Find meetings or online support.
  • NAR-Anon Family Groups: Provides support for those affected by someone else’s drug addiction.
  • Virtual NA Meetings: Search for online Narcotics Anonymous meetings held nationally.
  • One Promise Group of NA: Online Narcotics Anonymous support meetings.
  • Universal Hope Group of NA: Virtual NA group offering weekly recovery meetings.

Alcohol Recovery Support

  • NIAAA Alcohol Treatment Navigator: Helps adults find science-based alcohol treatment options for themselves or loved ones.
  • Alcoholics Anonymous (A.A.): A fellowship supporting people recovering from alcoholism. Search for local or online meetings.
  • Al-Anon Family Groups: Offers support for family and friends affected by someone’s drinking. Find in-person or virtual meetings.

Tobacco and Vaping Cessation

  • Smokefree.gov: Federal tools and personalized quit plans for tobacco and vaping cessation.
  • CDC Quit Smoking Resources: Evidence-based tips and guides from former smokers to help users quit for good.
  • FamilyDoctor.org - Tobacco Addiction: Offers medical insight and quitting strategies for tobacco use.
  • AAFP Tobacco Cessation Tools: Educational resources for quitting tobacco, provided by the American Academy of Family Physicians.
  • American Lung Association Lung HelpLine: Call 1-800-LUNGUSA (1-800-586-4872) for free counseling from respiratory therapists.
  • National Quitline: Call 1-800-QUIT-NOW (1-800-784-8669) to reach your state’s quitline for free help.
  • Illinois Tobacco Quitline: Free counseling and quitting tools for Illinois residents. Call 1-866-QUIT-YES.
  • You Can Quit Tobacco Guide: Practical advice and strategies to stop smoking from the American Cancer Society.

Accessing Free or Discounted Nicotine Patches

  • Use Your Insurance: Insurance may fully or partially cover nicotine patches with a prescription. If not covered in full, HSA or FSA funds may be used to offset remaining costs.
  • Check State Quitline Programs: Many states offer free nicotine patches through quitlines. Call 1-800-QUIT-NOW or text “QUITNOW” to 333888 for access to local programs and quit coaches.
  • GoodRx Nicotine Patch Coupons: Save up to 70% on generic nicotine patches at participating pharmacies. A prescription is required for over-the-counter use with a coupon.
  • How to Use a GoodRx Coupon for OTC Medications: Step-by-step guidance on using GoodRx for nicotine patches and other non-prescription products.
