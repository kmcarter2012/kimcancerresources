Connecticut Resources

Explore a variety of programs and services available specifically for Connecticut residents.

Connecticut Resource Search Portals

  • United Way 211 Connecticut: Statewide resource hotline and website connecting residents to health and human service needs. Call 211 for assistance.
  • FindHelp.org Connecticut: Search for free and reduced-cost services in your area across Connecticut.
  • MyPlaceCT: Connecticut's Aging and Disability Resource Center. Call 1-800-203-1234 for information and referrals.
  • ConneCT Portal: Apply for healthcare, food assistance, and other benefits through this state portal.
  • Connecticut Fair Housing Center: Centralized resource for housing assistance in Connecticut.

Connecticut Housing Resources

  • Connecticut Housing Finance Authority (CHFA): Connecticut's housing finance agency offering affordable housing programs, rental assistance, and homebuyer resources.
  • Rental Assistance Program: Comprehensive guide to rental assistance programs throughout Connecticut.
  • Home Repair Programs: Assistance for home modifications, repairs, and weatherization.
  • First-Time Homebuyer Program: Down payment assistance and affordable mortgages for first-time homebuyers.
  • Coordinated Access Network: Single point of entry for individuals and families experiencing homelessness.
  • Columbus House: Housing and supportive services for individuals and families experiencing homelessness.
  • Community Action Agencies: Housing assistance through local community action programs.
  • Local Initiatives Support Corporation (LISC): Support for community-based organizations providing affordable housing options.

Connecticut Utility Assistance

  • Connecticut Energy Assistance Program (CEAP): Helps low-income households with heating costs during winter months.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Free weatherization services to reduce energy costs for eligible households.
  • Eversource Payment Assistance Programs: Discount rates, budget billing, and payment plans for eligible customers.
  • Eversource Protection Programs: Shutoff protections for seniors, disabled individuals, and households with medical needs.
  • Community Action Agencies Utility Assistance: Emergency utility assistance through local CAP agencies.
  • Catholic Charities Emergency Utility Assistance: Help with utility bills for eligible households.
  • Salvation Army Emergency Assistance: Emergency utility assistance programs in Connecticut.
  • United Way Utility Bill Assistance: Referrals to utility assistance programs through 211.

Connecticut Food & Nutrition Resources

  • Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP): Food assistance benefits for eligible low-income individuals and families.
  • ConneCT - Apply for SNAP: Online portal to apply for SNAP benefits.
  • Connecticut Food Bank: Network of food pantries throughout the state. Call 203-469-5000 for assistance.
  • Women, Infants, and Children (WIC): Nutrition program for pregnant women, new mothers, and young children.
  • Commodity Supplemental Food Program: Monthly food packages for low-income seniors through local Community Action Agencies.
  • Senior Farmers Market Nutrition Program: Vouchers for seniors to purchase fresh produce at farmers markets.
  • Elderly Nutrition Program: Congregate meals at senior centers and home-delivered meals for eligible seniors.
  • Meals on Wheels Connecticut: Home-delivered meals for homebound seniors and adults with disabilities.
  • School Meals Programs: Free and reduced-price meals for eligible children in Connecticut schools.
  • Summer Meals Program: Free summer meals for children when school is not in session.

Connecticut Dental Resources

Explore dental care options and training-based clinics available across Connecticut.

  • Connecticut Department of Public Health Dental Clinics: Find low-cost dental providers throughout the state.
  • Federally Qualified Health Centers: Community health centers providing dental services to underserved populations.
  • Dental Lifeline Network (DLN): Free dental care for elderly, disabled, and medically compromised individuals who cannot afford treatment.
  • UConn School of Dental Medicine: Reduced-cost dental services at Connecticut's dental teaching facility.
  • Connecticut Dental Health Partnership: Dental services for HUSKY Health/Medicaid members.
  • Community Health Center, Inc. Dental Services: Sliding fee scale dental care for low-income patients.
  • Wheeler Clinic Dental Services: Comprehensive dental care with sliding fee discounts.
  • HUSKY Dental: Dental coverage for children and adults enrolled in Connecticut Medicaid.

Connecticut Senior Resources

Explore senior-focused services including benefits, caregiving support, and local aging resources.

Connecticut State Department on Aging and Disability Services

  • Department on Aging and Disability Services: Connecticut's state agency dedicated to serving older adults.
  • Programs and Services for Older Adults: Central resource for all aging programs and services.
  • Contact Information: Call 860-424-5055 to reach the main office for information and assistance.
  • MyPlaceCT: Connecticut's Aging and Disability Resource Center. Call 1-800-203-1234 for personalized assistance.
  • CHOICES Program: Health insurance assistance for Medicare beneficiaries.

Connecticut Senior Hotlines and Helplines

  • Elder Abuse/Neglect Reporting Line: Report elder abuse 24/7 at 1-888-385-4225.
  • Connecticut SHIP Medicare Counseling: Free Medicare assistance at 1-800-994-9422.
  • Office of the Healthcare Advocate: Call 1-866-466-4446 for health insurance questions.
  • CT Long-Term Care Ombudsman: Call 1-866-388-1888 for concerns about long-term care facilities.
  • United Way 2-1-1: Call 211 for 24/7 information and referrals to health and human services.
  • Mental Health Crisis Hotline: Call 1-800-HOPE-135 (1-800-467-3135) for mental health crisis assistance.
  • CT Food Bank Helpline: Call 203-469-5000 for food assistance information.

Connecticut Senior Benefits and Financial Assistance

  • Connecticut Energy Assistance Program (CEAP): Help with heating costs for eligible seniors.
  • Medicare Savings Program: Assistance with Medicare premiums for eligible seniors.
  • Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP): Food assistance with special rules for older adults.
  • Property Tax Credit: Tax credit for eligible elderly homeowners and renters.
  • Long Term Services and Supports: Financial assistance for long-term care needs.
  • Connecticut Legal Services: Free legal services for income-eligible seniors.
  • Senior Community Service Employment Program: Work opportunities for low-income older adults.
  • Eversource Discount Rates: Reduced utility rates for eligible seniors.

Connecticut Senior Healthcare Resources

  • CHOICES Program (SHIP): Free, unbiased Medicare counseling.
  • Access Health CT: Connecticut's health insurance marketplace for those under 65.
  • Connecticut Home Care Program for Elders: Home care services for eligible seniors.
  • Connecticut Prescription Assistance Program: Help with prescription drug costs.
  • Chronic Disease Self-Management Programs: Free workshops to help manage chronic conditions.
  • Fall Prevention Programs: Education and resources to prevent falls among seniors.
  • PACE (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly): Comprehensive care to help seniors remain at home.
  • Community Health Centers: Primary care services for seniors regardless of ability to pay.

Connecticut Senior Housing and Living Arrangements

  • CHFA Elderly Housing: Affordable housing options for Connecticut seniors.
  • Home Repair Programs: Assistance for home modifications and repairs for older homeowners.
  • Rental Assistance Program: Housing assistance for eligible seniors.
  • Senior Housing Options: Information on housing options and assistance programs.
  • Rental Housing Resources: Information on affordable rental options throughout Connecticut.
  • UniteCT: Rental and utility assistance for eligible Connecticut residents.
  • Long-Term Care Options: Information on nursing homes, assisted living, and other long-term care settings.
  • Partnership for Strong Communities: Coalition addressing housing needs in Connecticut.

Connecticut Senior Transportation

  • CT Transit Reduced Fare Program: Free or reduced fares for seniors on Connecticut public transit.
  • ADA Paratransit Service: ADA paratransit service for eligible seniors and people with disabilities.
  • Senior Transportation Programs: Various transportation assistance options for Connecticut seniors.
  • Non-Emergency Medical Transportation: Transportation for Medicaid recipients to medical appointments.
  • Alzheimer's Association Transportation Resources: Transportation services for individuals with dementia.
  • Eastern Connecticut Transportation Consortium: Transportation services in Eastern Connecticut communities.
  • Dial-A-Ride Services: Local transportation services for seniors in various Connecticut towns.
  • Community Action Agency Transportation: Transportation services through local community action agencies.

Connecticut Senior Nutrition Programs

  • Elderly Nutrition Program: Congregate and home-delivered meals for seniors 60+.
  • Connecticut Food Bank: Food pantries and meal sites throughout the state.
  • SNAP Benefits for Seniors: Special application process for adults 60+.
  • Commodity Supplemental Food Program: Monthly food boxes for low-income seniors.
  • Senior Farmers Market Nutrition Program: Vouchers for fresh produce at farmers markets.
  • Meals on Wheels Connecticut: Home-delivered meals for homebound seniors. Find your local program.
  • Healthy Living Collective Nutrition Services: Nutrition education and meal services for seniors with specialized diets.
  • End Hunger Connecticut: Resources to help seniors access nutritious food.

Connecticut Senior Activities and Engagement

  • Senior Centers Directory: Find local senior centers throughout Connecticut.
  • RSVP Volunteer Program: Volunteer opportunities for seniors 55 and older.
  • Age-Friendly Connecticut: Statewide initiative to create livable communities for older adults.
  • Osher Lifelong Learning Institute (OLLI): Educational programs for adults 50+ at University of Connecticut.
  • Hartford Department of Parks Recreation Senior Programs: Activities for seniors in Hartford.
  • CT Philanthropy Aging Initiatives: Programs promoting senior engagement and activity.
  • CT Office of the Arts - Creative Aging: Arts programs for older adults.
  • Connecticut Public Television Adult Learning: Educational resources and programming for older adults.

Connecticut Senior Technology Support

  • Digital Equity Programs: Technology training and support for older adults.
  • Senior Planet Connecticut: Free tech tutoring for seniors by trained mentors.
  • Senior Planet from AARP: Technology classes and resources for older adults.
  • Connecticut Public Libraries: Many offer computer classes and technology assistance.
  • Senior Community Service Employment Program (SCSEP): Includes technology training for employment.
  • Lifespan Connects: Telehealth and technology assistance for older patients.
  • Aging 2.0 Connecticut: Innovation in aging services including technology solutions.
  • CTDOT Technology for Seniors: Resources and programs related to technology access for seniors.

Connecticut Senior Legal Resources

  • Connecticut Legal Services: Free legal assistance for income-eligible seniors.

Hartford Resources

Specific resources for residents of Hartford, Connecticut.

  • Hartford Department of Parks Recreation Senior Programs: Activities and programs for seniors in Hartford.
  • Hartford Department of Housing & Community Development: Housing assistance programs within Hartford.

  • Hartford Food Assistance: Food resources and programs in Hartford.
