Indiana State Resource Guides

Comprehensive guides and directories provided by state agencies to help navigate available resources.

  • Indiana 2-1-1: A free service connecting Hoosiers with a wide range of human services, including housing, utilities, food, healthcare, and more. Dial 2-1-1 or visit the website 24/7.
  • Indiana Family and Social Services Administration (FSSA): The state agency for public assistance programs, including healthcare (Medicaid), food (SNAP), disability, aging, and mental health services.
  • FSSA Benefits Portal: Indiana's official online portal to apply for and manage benefits like SNAP (food assistance), TANF (cash assistance), and health coverage.
  • Indiana Bureau of Motor Vehicles (BMV) - ID Cards: Information on obtaining a state identification card, a crucial document for accessing many services.
  • Vocational Rehabilitation (VR): Helps individuals with disabilities prepare for, obtain, or retain employment.
  • Deaf and Hard of Hearing Services (DHHS): Provides resources and advocacy for deaf and hard of hearing individuals in Indiana.
  • Indiana Department of Veterans Affairs: Offers resources, benefits, and advocacy for veterans in Indiana. Phone: (317) 232-3910
  • Indiana Legal Help: Provides free legal information, self-help resources, and referrals to low-income Hoosiers with civil legal problems.
  • Indiana Assistance Programs: A comprehensive, privately managed list of assistance programs available to Indiana residents.

Indiana Housing Resources

  • Indiana Housing & Community Development Authority (IHCDA): The primary state agency for housing assistance, offering programs for rental assistance, homeownership, and foreclosure prevention. Phone: (317) 232-7777
  • U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) - Indiana: Federal housing resources, including information on rental assistance, Section 8 (Housing Choice Vouchers), and housing counseling.
  • Rental Assistance Programs: IHCDA's portal for various rental assistance programs, including information on the Housing Choice Voucher program.
  • Indiana Housing Now: A free state-supported service to help find affordable rental housing across Indiana.
  • Indiana Homelessness Resources: The Coalition for Homelessness Intervention & Prevention (CHIP) provides resources and information for those experiencing or at risk of homelessness.
  • Home Repair Assistance: Information on programs available through IHCDA and its partners to assist with necessary home repairs.

Indiana Utility Assistance

  • Low-Income Home Energy Assistance Program (LIHEAP): A federally funded program, also known as the Energy Assistance Program (EAP) in Indiana, that helps low-income households with their heating and cooling bills.
  • Low Income Household Water Assistance Program (LIHWAP): Provides financial assistance to eligible households for water and wastewater bills.
  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Helps reduce energy costs for low-income households by improving home energy efficiency.
  • 2-1-1 Utility Assistance: Connects you with local agencies and programs that provide utility assistance. Dial 2-1-1 for direct help.
  • Indiana Office of Utility Consumer Counselor (OUCC): Provides information on utility assistance programs offered by major utility companies across the state.

Indiana Food & Nutrition Resources

  • Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP): Indiana's food stamp program to help low-income families buy groceries. Apply through the FSSA Benefits Portal.
  • Indiana WIC Program: A nutrition program providing healthy food, nutrition education, and healthcare referrals for women, infants, and children up to age five. Phone: 1-800-522-0874
  • Feeding Indiana's Hungry: A statewide network of 11 food banks. Their website helps you find the food bank and local pantries serving your county.
  • Senior Nutrition Programs: FSSA's Division of Aging provides information on programs like congregate meals and home-delivered meals (Meals on Wheels).
  • School Meal Programs: The Indiana Department of Education oversees school breakfast, lunch, and summer food programs for children.

Indiana Dental Resources

Explore dental care options and training-based clinics available across Indiana.

  • Indiana Medicaid Dental Benefits: Information on dental services covered by Indiana Medicaid for eligible children and adults.
  • IU School of Dentistry Patient Care: Located in Indianapolis, the dental school offers comprehensive, patient-centered care at a reduced cost. Phone: (317) 274-7433
  • Indiana Department of Health - Oral Health Program: Provides information and resources for accessing dental care, including a list of safety-net dental clinics.
  • Federally Qualified Health Centers (FQHCs): Many FQHCs across Indiana offer dental services on a sliding fee scale based on income. Use the search tool to find one near you.
  • Community Compass Dental Search: Search for free or low-cost dental clinics, primarily in the Indianapolis area but with some statewide listings.

Indiana Cancer Financial Assistance

Financial resources specifically for cancer patients in Indiana.

  • Little Red Door Cancer Agency: Serving central Indiana, this agency provides financial assistance, emotional support, and navigation services to cancer patients. Phone: (317) 925-5595
  • American Cancer Society - Indiana: Offers patient support programs, including information on financial assistance, lodging, and transportation. 24/7 Helpline: 1-800-227-2345
  • Cancer Services of Northeast Indiana: Provides financial assistance, wellness programs, and a wig salon for cancer patients in Northeast Indiana. Phone: (260) 484-9560
  • Medicaid for Breast or Cervical Cancer (MBCC): A state program providing full health care coverage (including cancer treatment) to eligible individuals diagnosed with breast or cervical cancer.
  • CancerCare Financial Assistance Program: A national organization offering limited financial assistance for cancer-related costs. Phone: (800) 813-4673
  • HealthWell Foundation: A national foundation that provides financial assistance to help with prescription co-pays, treatment premiums, and other out-of-pocket costs.

Indiana Senior Resources

Explore support services for older adults in Indiana, including caregiving, nutrition, home care, food access, and transportation assistance.

Local Service Organizations (Southern Indiana)

Regional organizations serving Crawford, Floyd, Harrison, Scott, Washington, Clark, Lawrence, and Orange counties.

  • Hoosier Uplands Area XV Agency on Aging – In‑Home Services: Regional hub connecting seniors to in‑home care options. Provides homemaker & attendant care, consumer‑directed care, nursing/home‑health aid, Lifeline alerts, adult day care, meals, transportation, respite, assistive devices. Phone: 812‑849‑4457 or 1‑800‑333‑2451
  • Indiana CHOICE Program (Community & Home Options): State-funded Medicaid alternative providing comprehensive home services to prevent institutionalization. Apply through Hoosier Uplands at 812‑849‑4457
  • Medicaid Aged & Disabled Waiver (A&D Waiver): Medicaid waiver covering long‑term home care services to eligible seniors. Apply through Hoosier Uplands at 812‑849‑4457
  • Older Americans Services Corporation (OASC): Nonprofit providing homemaker, meals, and transportation to low-income seniors. Offers homemaker & attendant care, home-delivered and congregate meals, essential transport. Phone: 812‑865‑3352 or 1‑800‑258‑8786
  • Washington County Commission on Aging: Supports family caregivers through training, respite, and referrals. Provides short-term in-home respite, adult day referrals, caregiver education and support groups. Phone: 812‑849‑4457
  • Salvation Army – Southern Indiana (New Albany & Salem): Provides emergency aid, referrals, and occasional in‑home support. Offers emergency financial aid (rent/utilities), food, clothing, and referrals to in-home services. Phone: 812‑944‑1018 (New Albany office)
  • Southern Indiana Center for Independent Living (SICIL): Advocates for independent living and assists with home care access. Provides peer support, referrals (home health, homemaker), independent living skills, ombudsman services. Phone: 812‑277‑9626 or 1‑800‑845‑6914

Statewide Senior Resources

Indiana state programs and services available throughout the state.

  • FSSA Division of Aging: The main state portal for senior services, connecting to Area Agencies on Aging, elder abuse prevention, and in-home care services.
  • Indiana CHOICE Program (Community & Home Options): Provides in-home services and case management to help seniors and individuals with disabilities stay safely in their homes.
  • Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) - Aged & Disabled Waiver: Medicaid waiver services that provide personal care, home modifications, and supports for seniors who wish to remain in their homes.
  • Indiana Senior Meal Programs: Provides home-delivered and congregate meals for older adults through local Area Agencies on Aging.
  • Senior Farmers' Market Nutrition Program (SFMNP): Eligible low-income seniors can receive vouchers to purchase fresh fruits and vegetables at participating farmers markets.
  • Commodity Supplemental Food Program (CSFP): Monthly USDA food boxes for income-eligible seniors aged 60+ distributed through local food banks and agencies.
  • Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP): Food assistance for seniors; application support is available through Area Agencies on Aging.
  • Transportation Assistance: Connects seniors to local transit options and ride services for medical appointments and essential trips through Area Agencies on Aging.
  • Area Agencies on Aging (AAA): A network of 16 agencies across Indiana that are the primary access point for senior services. Call 1-800-986-3505 to be connected to your local office.
  • Division of Aging – Programs & Services: Overview of all aging programs including CHOICE, HCBS waivers, meal programs, caregiver support, and more.
  • Indiana Division of Aging – Contact & Senior Hotline: Call toll-free at 1-888-673-0002 for help connecting to services and resources across Indiana.
  • Adult Protective Services (APS): Investigates claims of abuse, neglect, or exploitation of endangered adults. Statewide Hotline: (800) 992-6978
  • State Health Insurance Assistance Program (SHIP): Free, unbiased counseling and information about Medicare, Medicare supplement insurance, and prescription drug coverage. Phone: 1-800-452-4800
  • Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly (PACE): A Medicare and Medicaid program that helps people meet their health care needs in the community instead of going to a nursing home or other care facility.

Indiana Transportation Resources

Explore transportation options for seniors, people with disabilities, and Medicaid recipients across Indiana.

  • Non-Emergency Medical Transportation (NEMT): A statewide program providing free transportation to and from medical appointments for eligible Medicaid members.
  • 2-1-1 Transportation Search: A comprehensive database of public transit, senior transportation, and medical transport options throughout Indiana.
  • Indiana Public Transit Systems: A list from INDOT of public transit providers by county, including rural and urban systems.
  • Area Agencies on Aging (AAA) Transportation: Many local AAA offices provide or coordinate transportation services for seniors. Call 1-800-986-3505 to find your local provider.
  • American Cancer Society Road to Recovery: Volunteer drivers provide free rides to and from cancer treatment for patients who have no other means of transportation. Phone: 1-800-227-2345

Indiana Medical Equipment & Incontinence Supplies

Resources for accessing medical equipment loan closets, incontinence supplies, and other medical necessities across Indiana.

  • Medicaid - Durable Medical Equipment (DME): Information on how Indiana Medicaid covers necessary medical equipment and supplies for eligible members.
  • PATINS Project - Refurbished Equipment: While focused on education, PATINS offers some refurbished assistive technology and may have information on other programs.
  • Easterseals Crossroads (Central Indiana): Offers an equipment loan library and reuse program for assistive technology and medical equipment. Phone: (317) 466-2013
  • 2-1-1 Assistive Technology Search: Use the 2-1-1 database to search for medical equipment loan closets or assistive technology providers in your specific area.
  • The Arc of Indiana: An advocacy organization for people with intellectual and developmental disabilities that can often provide referrals to equipment resources. Phone: (317) 977-2375

Indianapolis / Marion County Resources

Explore support services available to residents of Indianapolis and Marion County.

General & Financial Assistance

  • United Way of Central Indiana 2-1-1: Connects residents to essential health and human services, including shelter, food, and financial assistance.
  • Community Action of Greater Indianapolis (CAGI): Offers programs to address the causes of poverty, including energy assistance, family development, and more. Phone: (317) 396-1800

Housing & Homelessness

  • Indianapolis Housing Agency (IHA): Administers public housing and the Housing Choice Voucher (Section 8) program for Marion County. Phone: (317) 261-7200
  • Coalition for Homelessness Intervention & Prevention (CHIP): The lead agency coordinating homeless services and prevention in Indianapolis.

Food Assistance

  • Gleaners Food Bank of Indiana: The primary food bank serving Central Indiana. Use their website to find local food pantries and mobile distributions. Phone: (317) 925-0191

Transportation

  • IndyGo: The public transportation provider for Indianapolis, offering fixed bus routes and the Red Line BRT. Phone: (317) 635-3344
  • IndyGo Access (Open Door): Paratransit service for individuals with disabilities who cannot use fixed-route buses.

Fort Wayne / Allen County Resources

Resources specifically for residents of Fort Wayne and Allen County areas.

  • Brightpoint: A community action agency providing a wide range of services, including utility assistance, housing, healthcare enrollment, and childcare. Phone: (260) 423-3546
  • City of Fort Wayne - Financial Assistance: Portal for city-run assistance programs, often including rent and utility help.
  • Fort Wayne Housing Authority: Manages public housing and the Section 8 Housing Choice Voucher program in Fort Wayne. Phone: (260) 267-9300
  • Community Harvest Food Bank of Northeast Indiana: The primary food bank for the region. Use their website to find local food pantries. Phone: (260) 447-3696
  • Citilink: The public transit provider for Fort Wayne. They also offer Citilink Access for eligible riders with disabilities. Phone: (260) 432-4546
  • Cancer Services of Northeast Indiana: Provides financial assistance, wellness programs, and a wig salon for cancer patients in the area. Phone: (260) 484-9560

Evansville / Vanderburgh County Resources

Explore support services available to residents of Evansville and Vanderburgh County.

  • Community Action Program of Evansville (CAPE): The local community action agency offering energy assistance, weatherization, and other self-sufficiency programs. Phone: (812) 425-4241
  • Evansville Housing Authority: Manages public housing and Section 8 housing programs for the city. Phone: (812) 428-8500
  • Tri-State Food Bank: Serves 33 counties in Indiana, Illinois, and Kentucky from its Evansville base. Phone: (812) 425-0775
  • Metropolitan Evansville Transit System (METS): Public transportation for the Evansville area, including mobility paratransit services. Phone: (812) 435-6166
  • Aurora Evansville: An organization dedicated to preventing and ending homelessness in the region. Phone: (812) 428-3246

South Bend / St. Joseph County Resources

Explore support services available to residents of South Bend and St. Joseph County.

  • REAL Services, Inc.: The Area Agency on Aging for the region, but also provides a wide range of community services for all ages, including energy assistance, transportation, and nutrition programs. Phone: (574) 233-8205
  • Housing Authority of South Bend (HASB): Manages public and voucher-based housing programs. Phone: (574) 235-9346
  • Food Bank of Northern Indiana: The central food bank for the region, supplying local pantries and meal sites. Phone: (574) 232-9986
  • South Bend Public Transportation (Transpo): Provides fixed-route bus service in the South Bend/Mishawaka area, as well as paratransit services. Phone: (574) 233-2131
  • St. Joseph County Department of Human Services: County office providing referrals and information on various assistance programs.

Northwest Indiana / Lake County Resources

Resources specifically for residents of Northwest Indiana, including Lake, Porter, and surrounding counties.

  • Northwest Indiana Community Action (NWICA): The primary community action agency and Area Agency on Aging, providing energy assistance, senior meals, housing services, and more. Phone: (219) 794-1829
  • Food Bank of Northwest Indiana: Distributes food to partner agencies across Lake and Porter counties. Phone: (219) 980-1777
  • Gary Community Services Division: Offers assistance programs, including energy and emergency help, for residents of Gary. Phone: (219) 881-4886
  • East Chicago & Hammond Housing Authorities: These cities operate their own housing authorities for public housing and voucher programs.
  • South Shore Line: Commuter rail service connecting South Bend, Gary, Hammond, and East Chicago with Chicago.
  • Gary Public Transportation Corp. (GPTC): The primary bus service for Gary and surrounding communities. Phone: (219) 884-6100
