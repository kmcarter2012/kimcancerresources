New Jersey Resources

Explore a variety of programs and services available specifically for New Jersey seniors, including statewide resources for housing, utilities, food assistance, and transportation.

New Jersey Resource Directories

Comprehensive resource directories to help connect New Jersey residents with health and social services.

  • Horizon Neighbors in Health (Horizon Blue Cross Blue Shield of New Jersey): Connects individuals to community resources related to health, social services, and well-being.
  • AtlantiCare Community Resources: Links patients and community members in the AtlantiCare service area to supportive services such as food, housing, and transportation.
  • Hackensack Meridian Health Community Resources (Unite Us): A searchable platform linking individuals and families with community-based resources for food, housing, transportation, utilities assistance, and other social services in New Jersey.
  • Atlantic Health System Community Resources: Provides a searchable platform connecting individuals and families with community-based resources for food, housing, transportation, utilities assistance, and other social services in New Jersey.
  • NJ 2-1-1: General statewide resource and referral line, accessible by phone (dial 211) or online.
  • NJ Aging & Disability Resource Connection: For older adults and people with disabilities.
  • NJHelps: Online screening tool for various state services, including food, housing, and cash assistance.
  • NJ Resources 2024 (New Jersey Department of Human Services): Comprehensive guide to statewide resources including health, housing, food, and disability services.
  • Aging & Disability Resource Connection of New Jersey (ADRCNJ): Provides information and assistance for seniors and individuals with disabilities in New Jersey. Helps connect users to resources for healthcare, housing, transportation, home-delivered meals, and caregiving services. The site also offers a directory of local Area Agencies on Aging (AAA) for county-specific services. Phone: 1-877-222-3737
  • USA.gov Benefit Finder: Helps individuals find federal and state benefits, including healthcare assistance, food programs, housing support, and financial aid. Users can complete an online questionnaire to identify eligibility for various programs, including support for medical expenses and cancer treatments.
  • BenefitsCheckUp: A tool by the National Council on Aging to help older adults and individuals with disabilities find programs to assist with healthcare, medication, food, utilities, and other expenses.
  • Community Resource Finder: Provides an online directory to help individuals find local community services, including healthcare providers, housing options, home care, elder law attorneys, and support resources for seniors and caregivers. Operated by AARP and the Alzheimer's Association.
  • American Cancer Society - New Jersey: Provides support for cancer patients, including transportation assistance, lodging during treatment, support groups, educational resources, and patient navigation services.

New Jersey Resource Search Portals

  • New Jersey Division of Aging Services: The state agency that administers federal and state-funded programs to help older adults live in the community with independence, dignity, and choice.
  • NJHelps: A screening tool to check eligibility for food assistance (SNAP), cash assistance, and health insurance programs.
  • Aging and Disability Resource Center (ADRC): A statewide network providing information and assistance for seniors, people with disabilities, and caregivers.
  • NJ 2-1-1: A comprehensive resource for connecting people to community resources, including food, housing, utilities, and more. Call 2-1-1 for immediate assistance.

New Jersey Housing Resources

  • State Rental Assistance Program (SRAP): Provides housing subsidies for very low-income New Jersey residents. Preferences are given to veterans, homeless individuals, elderly, and disabled people.
  • Department of Community Affairs: Provides administrative guidance, financial support, and technical assistance for housing needs.
  • Low-Income Housing Assistance: Directory of low-income assistance and resources for residents throughout New Jersey.
  • Housing Assistance Programs: State resources for various housing needs including the NJ Housing Resource Center.
  • Emergency Rental Assistance Programs: Assistance for rent and utilities to eligible residents across New Jersey.

New Jersey Utility Assistance

  • PSE&G Payment Assistance Programs: Offers NJ Lifeline, a yearly utility credit for seniors over 65 and disabled adults. Income limits apply.
  • Utility Assistance Programs: Multiple state programs for customers struggling to pay energy bills, including LIHEAP and others.
  • NJ Save: Online application to help low-income seniors save money on utilities, Medicare premiums, prescription costs, and other living expenses.
  • The Salvation Army Utility Assistance: Emergency utility assistance for eligible residents facing disconnection.

New Jersey Food & Nutrition Resources

  • NJ SNAP: New Jersey's Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program provides food assistance to low-income families through a benefits card.
  • Food Assistance Programs: Multiple resources for immediate and long-term food needs, including food pantries, SNAP, and home-delivered meals.
  • Community FoodBank of New Jersey: Connects individuals with resources for free food and other services through partner agencies across the state.
  • Food Bank of South Jersey: Provides food assistance to residents in Burlington, Camden, Gloucester, and Salem counties.
  • Fulfill NJ: Serves Monmouth and Ocean counties with food distribution and SNAP enrollment assistance.
  • Get Food This Week: Information on food pantries and soup kitchens for immediate food needs across the state.

New Jersey Dental Resources

Explore dental care options and training-based clinics available across New Jersey.

  • New Jersey Dental Association - Find a Dentist: Directory of dentists and clinics providing care for patients with limited financial resources.
  • New Jersey Federally Qualified Health Centers: Health centers providing dental care for low-income, uninsured, and underinsured patients.
  • Rutgers School of Dental Medicine: Dental care provided by dental students under supervision. Phone: (973) 972-4300
  • University of Medicine and Dentistry of New Jersey: Low-cost dental care through dental school clinics. Phone: (973) 972-4300
  • New Jersey Health Connect: Network of community health centers providing dental services on a sliding fee scale.
