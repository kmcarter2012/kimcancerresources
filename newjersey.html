New Jersey Resources

Explore a variety of programs and services available specifically for New Jersey seniors, including statewide resources for housing, utilities, food assistance, and transportation.

New Jersey Resource Directories

Comprehensive resource directories to help connect New Jersey residents with health and social services.

  • Horizon Neighbors in Health (Horizon Blue Cross Blue Shield of New Jersey): Connects individuals to community resources related to health, social services, and well-being.
  • AtlantiCare Community Resources: Links patients and community members in the AtlantiCare service area to supportive services such as food, housing, and transportation.
  • Hackensack Meridian Health Community Resources (Unite Us): A searchable platform linking individuals and families with community-based resources for food, housing, transportation, utilities assistance, and other social services in New Jersey.
  • Atlantic Health System Community Resources: Provides a searchable platform connecting individuals and families with community-based resources for food, housing, transportation, utilities assistance, and other social services in New Jersey.
  • NJ 2-1-1: General statewide resource and referral line, accessible by phone (dial 211) or online.
  • NJ Aging & Disability Resource Connection (ADRC): For older adults and people with disabilities. (Also listed below under Senior Resources and Search Portals)
  • NJHelps: Online screening tool for various state services, including food, housing, and cash assistance. (Also listed below under Search Portals and Food Assistance)
  • NJ Resources 2024 (New Jersey Department of Human Services): Comprehensive guide to statewide resources including health, housing, food, and disability services.
  • USA.gov Benefit Finder: Helps individuals find federal and state benefits, including healthcare assistance, food programs, housing support, and financial aid.
  • BenefitsCheckUp: A tool by the National Council on Aging to help older adults and individuals with disabilities find programs to assist with healthcare, medication, food, utilities, and other expenses.
  • Community Resource Finder: Provides an online directory to help individuals find local community services. Operated by AARP and the Alzheimer's Association.
  • American Cancer Society - New Jersey: Provides support for cancer patients, including transportation assistance, lodging during treatment, support groups, and educational resources.

New Jersey Resource Search Portals

  • New Jersey Division of Aging Services: The state agency that administers federal and state-funded programs to help older adults. (Also listed under Senior Resources)
  • NJHelps: A screening tool to check eligibility for food assistance (SNAP), cash assistance, and health insurance programs.
  • Aging and Disability Resource Center (ADRC): A statewide network providing information and assistance for seniors, people with disabilities, and caregivers.
  • NJ 2-1-1: A comprehensive resource for connecting people to community resources. Call 2-1-1 for immediate assistance.

New Jersey Housing Resources

  • State Rental Assistance Program (SRAP): Provides housing subsidies for very low-income New Jersey residents. Preferences are given to veterans, homeless individuals, elderly, and disabled people.
  • Department of Community Affairs: Provides administrative guidance, financial support, and technical assistance for housing needs.
  • Low-Income Housing Assistance (MoneyFit): Directory of low-income assistance and resources for residents throughout New Jersey.
  • Housing Assistance Programs (State of NJ): State resources for various housing needs including the NJ Housing Resource Center.
  • Emergency Rental Assistance Programs (ERAP): Assistance for rent and utilities to eligible residents across New Jersey.

New Jersey Utility Assistance

  • PSE&G Payment Assistance Programs: Offers various programs including NJ Lifeline.
  • Lifeline Utility Assistance Program: Offers a $225 annual credit for electric and gas utilities for eligible low-income seniors and individuals with disabilities. Phone: 1-800-792-9745.
  • Utility Assistance Programs (NJ BPU): Multiple state programs for customers struggling to pay energy bills, including LIHEAP and others.
  • NJ Save: Online application to help low-income seniors save money on utilities, Medicare premiums, prescription costs, and other living expenses. (The main NJSave application portal)
  • The Salvation Army Utility Assistance: Emergency utility assistance for eligible residents facing disconnection.

New Jersey Food & Nutrition Resources

This section provides links to general food assistance programs, major food banks, county-specific resources, and meal delivery services.

General Food Assistance

  • NJ SNAP: New Jersey's Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program provides food assistance to low-income families through a benefits card.
  • Food Assistance Programs (NJ Dept. of Human Services): Multiple resources for immediate and long-term food needs, including food pantries, SNAP, and home-delivered meals.
  • NJHelps: A New Jersey-specific tool that allows residents to screen for eligibility and apply for programs such as SNAP, NJ FamilyCare/Medicaid, cash assistance, and more.
  • USDA National Hunger Hotline: Provides information on food resources such as meal sites, food banks, and other social services available nationwide. Phone: 1-866-3-HUNGRY.
  • WhyHunger Hotline: Connects individuals to food pantries, soup kitchens, summer meal sites, and government nutrition programs. Phone: 1-800-5-HUNGRY.
  • Feeding America: Nationwide network of food banks.
  • findhelp.org: Interactive platform to search and connect to support, including food pantries.
  • USAGov - Emergency Food Assistance: Information on immediate food assistance.
  • Nutrition.gov - Food Assistance Programs: Information on USDA commodity distribution programs, nutrition programs for seniors, and SNAP.
  • USDA Local Food Directories: Search for local food businesses, including farmers' markets.
  • Get Food This Week (State of NJ): Information on food pantries and soup kitchens for immediate food needs across the state.

Larger Food Banks

  • Community FoodBank of New Jersey (CFBNJ): Connects individuals with resources for free food and other services through partner agencies across the state. Locations in Hillside and Egg Harbor Township. Phone: 908-355-3663.
  • Food Bank of South Jersey: Provides food assistance to residents in Burlington, Camden, Gloucester, and Salem counties. Phone: 856-662-4884.
  • Fulfill (Monmouth and Ocean Counties): Serves Monmouth and Ocean counties with food distribution and SNAP enrollment assistance. Phone: 732-918-2600.
  • Mercer Street Friends Food Bank (Mercer County): Regional food bank providing food and nutrition education. Phone: 609-406-0503.
  • NORWESCAP Food Bank (Hunterdon, Sussex, Warren Counties): Distributes food to families in Northwest New Jersey. Phone: 908-454-4322.

New Jersey Senior Resources

Explore senior-focused services including benefits, caregiving support, and local aging resources.

New Jersey Division of Aging Services

  • General Information & Medical Counseling: Phone: 1-800-792-8820
  • NJ Save Hotline (PAAD, Sr. Gold, Medicare Saving Programs, Lifeline): Phone: 1-800-792-9745
  • Website: Division of Aging Services
  • Aging & Disability Resource Connection (ADRC) Statewide Toll-Free: Phone: 1-877-222-3737 or visit ADRCNJ Website

General Senior Programs & Support

  • Jersey Assistance for Community Caregiving (JACC): In-home services for seniors 60+ needing nursing facility-level care. Contact County ADRC.
  • Congregate or Group Meals / Home-Delivered Meals (State Info): Nutritious meals for individuals 60+. Contact County Area Agency on Aging (AAA).
  • Statewide Respite Care Program: Respite care for unpaid caregivers. Contact County ADRC.
  • Senior Farmers' Market Nutrition Program (SFMNP): Vouchers for fresh produce for low-income seniors. Contact County ADRC.
  • Pharmaceutical Assistance to the Aged and Disabled (PAAD): Financial assistance for prescriptions. Phone: 1-800-792-9745.
  • Senior Gold Prescription Discount Program: Prescription discounts for seniors not qualifying for PAAD. Phone: 1-800-792-9745.
  • Personal Assistance Services Program (PASP): Non-medical personal care for individuals with permanent physical disabilities. Contact County PASP Coordinator.
  • New Jersey Advocates for Aging Well (NJAAW): Comprehensive list of services and resources for seniors. Phone: 609-421-0206.

Area Agencies on Aging (AAA)

  • Area Agencies on Aging (AAA) provide localized services and support for seniors. To find your local AAA, please visit the Aging & Disability Resource Connection of New Jersey (ADRCNJ) website, which includes a directory, or call 1-877-222-3737.

Medication Assistance

Resources for help with prescription costs, co-pays, and finding assistance programs.

General Prescription Assistance

  • Pharmaceutical Assistance to the Aged and Disabled (PAAD): Helps eligible NJ residents pay for prescriptions. Phone: 1-800-792-9745.
  • Senior Gold Prescription Discount Program: For NJ residents who meet income limits but are not eligible for PAAD. Phone: 1-800-792-9745.
  • GoodRx for Healthcare Professionals: Tools to help patients save on medications.
  • Medicine Assistance Tool (MAT): Search engine for prescription assistance programs.
  • GoodRx - What to Do When Your Prescription Drug Isn't Covered: Tips and strategies.
  • NeedyMeds: Prescription drug assistance and discount card.
  • NJ Hospital Charity Care Program: Covers medically necessary services for uninsured/underinsured. Phone: 1-866-588-5696.

  • Cancer Hope Network: One-on-one support and information on financial resources. Phone: 1-877-467-3638.
  • American Cancer Society (NJ Division): Educational resources, patient navigation, rides, lodging. Phone: 1-800-227-2345.

  • The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society (LLS) Patient Financial Assistance: Copay assistance, grants for transportation, lodging. Phone: 1-800-955-4572.
  • Leukemia & Lymphoma Society (LLS) Co-Pay Assistance Program: Financial support for blood cancer patients. Phone: 1-877-557-2672.
  • The Patient Advocate Foundation (PAF) Co-Pay Relief Program: Direct financial support for eligible patients. Phone: 1-866-512-3861.
  • Patient Advocate Foundation (PAF): Co-pay relief and financial aid, case management. Phone: 1-800-532-5274.
  • The Samfund (via Expect Miracles Foundation): Financial assistance for young adult cancer survivors.
  • HealthWell Foundation: Financial assistance for premiums, deductibles, co-pays, travel. Phone: 1-800-675-8416.
  • CancerCare - Financial Assistance: Help with transportation, home care, child care, co-pays. Phone: 1-800-813-4673.
  • PAN Foundation: Financial assistance for out-of-pocket expenses. Phone: 1-866-316-7263.
